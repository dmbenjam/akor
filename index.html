A.K.O.R. (Ankourajman, Konsekans, Onè, Respè) is a culturally relevant parenting education program designed for Haiti and its diaspora.

The Challenge

"Haitian parents face a collision of cultures — without the tools to navigate it."

The cultural transition. Parents raised in authoritarian traditions struggle to adapt discipline methods to the expectations of democratic societies, where open dialogue and child agency are the norm.

The consequences. Without guidance, families face court referrals for discipline, broken trust between parents and children, and disengagement from schools that don't share their language or context.

The gap. There is a shortage of culturally relevant, Haitian-led parenting resources in Creole — materials that meet families where they are.

Our Founder

GB

Dr. Guilhène Benjamin, Founder

Dr. Guilhène Benjamin began her career at a young age and has drawn from enriching experiences in both her home country of Haiti and the United States. Now retired, she devotes her time to pursuing an ideal she formed early in her career: connecting Haitian parents with resources in their home language, helping them transition from the authoritarian traditions of their upbringing to the democratic culture of their host society. Dr. Benjamin developed the AKOR parenting education program through years of action research with parents in Miami-Dade schools. She is the proud founder of the nonprofit AKOR Parenting, which she leads in collaboration with educators, psychologists, and university partners across Miami and Haiti.
